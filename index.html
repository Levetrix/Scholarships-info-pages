BYU Independent Study’s High School Scholarship Program

What is the High School Scholarship Program?

Our new scholarship program benefits all middle and high school students. It allows them to receive financial assistance—without requiring their schools to have previously accrued multiple enrollments. This scholarship program is now completely based on financial need and academic achievement. We look forward to rewarding more students with scholarships for online courses and providing our valued school counselors the ability to recommend students they feel are deserving.

How does a counselor submit a scholarship application for a student?

The application process is designed to be simple and easy to understand. To make it even easier, the following lists are provided to help you in the process.

Information needed before filling out the application form:

  • Counselor BYU Net ID (username): click here for a tutorial to create one.
  • Course in which the student wants to enroll. To view our course catalog, click here.
  • Student BYU Net ID: this allows us to attach each student’s records to the school and the course (if rewarded a scholarship). The counselor or student can create a BYU Net ID by going here.

How to fill out the application form:

  • The counselor will go to byu.is/scholarships and click “Sign in with a BYU Net ID.”
  • Click on the “Apply for Scholarship” button in the top left-hand corner of the page. This will bring up the scholarship application.
  • The following is a list of what information is needed to fill out the form:
    • The school’s name and state.
    • Student’s full name and BYU Net ID.
    • The course title.
    • Student’s approximate GPA.
    • Student’s financial needs.
    • Guidance counselor’s recommendation.
  • Once the form is filled out, simply click on the “SUBMIT” button.

The review process is also simple. Once the application has been electronically submitted, it will be evaluated by a scholarship committee who reviews applications on a weekly basis. Both the counselor and student will receive an email notifying them whether or not the application has been approved.

What criteria must my student meet to receive a scholarship?

It’s simple: if you feel your student has financial need or shows academic achievement, apply! We know each student’s situation is unique, so tell us why you are recommending your student to receive a scholarship. Schools will need one previous enrollment with BYU Independent Study to be eligible to apply for a course scholarship for a student.

Why do you offer partial and full scholarships?

Our scholarship program is designed with the student’s best interest in mind. We have found that when there is a financial obligation associated with a course, students tend to not only complete the course, but they also put forth more effort, perform higher academically, and obtain more parental involvement. For this reason, there is an option of receiving a partial or full scholarship.

How is this different from the Course Awards Program?

The course awards program is exclusively directed toward those students admitted into one of our full-time high school programs (either the Standard, Advanced, or Adult Diploma). They can receive one free scholarship for every five courses they complete with a B or higher grade. For more information, click here. Note: students in our full-time programs are not eligible for our High School Scholarship program.
